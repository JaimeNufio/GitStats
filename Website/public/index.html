Spotify Statistics

Understand the underlying statistics that define how Spotify's algorithims interpret your music, and what they might say about you. Using Spotify's API, we'll average values of your library's music and learn the quantitative aspects of your saved.

Tempo:

Beats per minute, aka the songs pace.

Danceability:

How suitable a song is for dancing based on tempo, rhythm, beat, and how consistent those are throughout the track.

Energy:

Percieved intensity and activity. Energetic tracks are fast, loud, and noisey. Attributes that contribute include dynamic range, loudness, timbre, onset rate, and general entropy.

Valence:

Valence describes positivity in the track. A track with a high score is more closely associated with postive feelings like happiness, while a track with a low score is more associated with negative feelings like saddness.

Acousticness:

How much the algorithim believes this track is accoustic. Consequently, a measure of acoustic attributes to the track. A value greater than 50% means the song is most likely accoustic.

Loudness:

How loud the track is. This scale starts around -20dB and goes upto 0dB. Loudness is correlated to physical power of the song, as in amplitude.
